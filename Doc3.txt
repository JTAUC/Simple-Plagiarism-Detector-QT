
Climate change has been a topic of debate for years. Some believe it is a natural phenomenon, while others believe that human activity is the main cause. If you exclude all the evidence about climate change and global warming that is presented to us on a daily basis, then if you look at the weather today compared to decades ago, you can see a change in the climate . One of the most significant impacts of climate change is changing weather patterns. Rising temperatures have led to more frequent and severe heat waves, while winters have become milder.
This change in weather patterns has major implications for agriculture, as crops need specific weather conditions to thrive. Additionally, changes in weather patterns could lead to water shortages in some areas, which could have serious consequences for humans and wildlife. Climate change is a real phenomenon that refers to long-term changes in weather patterns and global temperature, mainly caused by human activities such as burning fossil fuels and deforestation. The scientific consensus is clear: the Earth is warming, largely due to human activity. Here are some reasons why climate change is real.
